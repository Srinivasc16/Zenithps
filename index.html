Health Tech

Smart Pharmacy Inventory

Intelligent forecasting and expiry management to prevent medicine waste and optimize stock levels.

Baseline Requirements

Demand Forecasting

Predicts future medicine requirements using past sales, seasonal patterns, and trends.

Scenario

During peak monsoon season, customers frequently purchase Paracetamol and ORS, causing sudden demand spikes.

System Response: Analyzes seasonality and trends to recommend optimal reorder quantities.
Real-Time Alerts

Sends alerts for low stock, expiry warnings, and reorder needs.

Scenario

Vitamin-D and immunity boosters drop below safety stock during high traffic; staff may not notice.

System Response: Triggers instant low-stock alerts and highlights priority items.
Smart Shelf (FEFO)

Applies First-Expired-First-Out logic, tracks expiring batches, and manages returns.

Scenario

A batch of Amoxicillin expires in 15 days; staff might overlook it leading to loss.

System Response: Marks batch as "Expiring Soon," enforces FEFO picking, and suggests return.
Chatbot Assistance

Answers queries like stock availability and expiry details instantly.

Scenario

Staff asks: "How many ORS packets expire in the next 30 days?" while handling a customer.

System Response: Chatbot instantly retrieves data and answers without manual search.

Edge Cases

Edge Case Description Occurs When Expected System Behaviour
Medicines close to expiry should be used first. Multiple batches of same medicine with different expiry dates are available in inventory. System always suggests stock from the batch with the earliest expiry date and warns if a batch is nearing expiry.
Demand forecasting for price surge of a drug. Historical data predicts a peak in the price of a particular drug soon. System flags the drug, shows an alert to stock up, and possibly suggests an optimal reorder quantity before price hike.
Spike in transaction history for a specific drug during certain time periods. Repeated pattern of high sales volume of a drug in specific time windows (e.g., seasonal). System detects the pattern, shows alerts, and suggests adjusting stock levels to ensure availability.
Alternative company suggestions when a drug brand is out of stock. Drug from a company is out of stock, but an equivalent drug from another company exists. System suggests equivalent drug from other companies and clearly indicates substitution to the pharmacist.
Chatbot answering only pharmacy-relevant queries. User asks the chatbot unrelated questions instead of pharmaceutical queries. Chatbot is restricted to only pharmacy-related information and informs user that it cannot handle unrelated queries.
Acknowledgement for low-supply drug requests sent to manager. Pharmacist raises a request for a drug that is low in stock. System confirms submission (e.g., success message), or shows a clear error if request fails; manager also gets a notification.

The edge cases mentioned above are only for understanding the idea. More edge cases and system behavior details will be given on the day of the hackathon.

Education

Academic & Exam Administration System

Streamlining exams, seating allocation, and campus events with intelligent automation.

Baseline Requirements

Role-Based Login

Personalized dashboards for students, faculty, and admins.

Scenario

A student or faculty member logs in to access their specific features.

System Response: Identifies role and shows only relevant modules and permissions.
Mind Map Helper

Converts syllabus PDFs into mind maps and links study resources.

Scenario

Student uploads a syllabus PDF to revise topics for mid-sem exams.

System Response: Scans doc, extracts topics, generates mind map, and links materials.
Hall Ticket Generation

Auto-generates tickets with QR/barcodes and schedules.

Scenario

Exam dates finalized; students need official tickets with seat numbers.

System Response: Auto-generates downloadable tickets with QR codes and mapped schedules.
Seating Allocation

Randomly assigns seats and generates charts.

Scenario

300 students must be arranged across 6 halls with spacing.

System Response: Allocates seats intelligently and generates charts for invigilators.

Innovation Bonus

Add your own creative features beyond the requirements! Additional scores will be awarded for innovative and useful implementations.

Edge Cases

Edge Case Description Trigger Condition Expected System Behaviour
Handling students who fail after starting new academic year (credit shortage). Student is promoted to next academic year, then results are updated and they fail in a few subjects causing credit shortage. System re-evaluates eligibility, updates academic status, recalculates credits, reflects the changes in the student portal and reports to administration.
Mapping bulk-uploaded hall tickets to correct students. Management uploads hall tickets in bulk per branch, with the file named by roll number. System matches hall tickets to students by roll number, attaches them to the correct profile, and makes them downloadable from the student's login.
Management acknowledgement of hall ticket delivery status. After bulk upload, some hall tickets fail to attach due to errors (wrong file name format, missing roll numbers, etc.). System displays a branch-wise delivery report showing success/failure and lists roll numbers needing re-upload.
Seat allocation behaviour for detained students. Detained students exist in the database during seat allocation. System excludes detained students from seat allocation, and blocks seat number generation unless their status is corrected by admin.
Flexibility for clubs to update event details. A club event is scheduled, but later needs to be cancelled or rescheduled (date/venue/time changes). System allows authorized club coordinators to edit event details, notify registered participants of changes/cancellations, and updates the event calendar accordingly.

The edge cases mentioned above are only for understanding the idea. More edge cases and system behavior details will be given on the day of the hackathon.

AgriTech

Smart Farming System

Location-based data and soil analysis for maximizing crop yield and sustainable farming.

Baseline Requirements

Location Detection

Identifies location even in weak GPS areas.

Scenario

Farmer lives in a remote village where GPS is poor.

System Response: Enables manual village/district selection to identify land.
Soil Type ID

Determines soil type using region and geo-data.

Scenario

Farmer wants to understand soil composition before selecting crops.

System Response: Matches coordinates to database and displays soil characteristics.
Season Analysis

Analyzes rainfall and weather to reduce crop failure.

Scenario

Forecasts indicate unusual rainfall next month.

System Response: Adjusts crop, fertilizer, and irrigation advice based on prediction.
Crop Recommendation

Suggests best crops for soil/climate to lower risk.

Scenario

Farmer is unsure which crop delivers best yield currently.

System Response: Recommends optimal crops with high yield potential.

Innovation Bonus

Add your own creative features beyond the requirements! Additional scores will be awarded for innovative and useful implementations.

Edge Cases

Edge Case Description Trigger Condition Expected System Behaviour
Suggesting cheaper fertilizer combinations. System recommends some fertilizer but user wants a lower-cost option. System suggests a mix of alternative fertilizers with similar nutrient composition and shows cost comparison.
Handling GPS detection errors. GPS signal is weak, inaccurate, or unavailable. System detects GPS issues, shows an error/warning, and allows manual location input or retry.
Accepting detailed soil input from user. System auto-detects soil type but user has more specific soil information. System allows user to enter or refine soil details (e.g., texture) and updates recommendations accordingly.
Adapting to yearly temperature changes. Historical patterns change and yearly temperatures differ from previous years. System uses updated weather/climate data each year and adjusts crop and fertilizer analysis based on current trends.
Providing recommendations when crop is not in system database. Farmer selects or enters a crop that is rare or not listed in the system. System asks for crop details (growth duration, water needs, soil preference) and generates a basic recommendation or suggests the closest known crop profile.

The edge cases mentioned above are only for understanding the idea. More edge cases and system behavior details will be given on the day of the hackathon.
